Bill Zheng

I am a third-year undergraduate student at UC Berkeley studying Electrical Enginerring and Computer Science (EECS). I am currently conducting research under advisory of Prof. Jitendra Malik at Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Group (BAIR), specializing in computer vision models for robotics. Previously I worked with Prof. Mark Vogelsberger at MIT's physics department in representation learning.

Broadly speaking, I am interested in skill-discovery: how can autonomous agents learn policies in real-world that resemble or sometimes exceed human performance with little training? By extension, I am also interested in machine learning theory, espeically in the field of in-context learning and meta learning, and any few-shot learning techniques that are transferrable to real world.
